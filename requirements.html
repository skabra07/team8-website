Project Background

In the UK, more children die of preventable illnesses than elsewhere in Europe and this is due to the lack of information about these illnesses. We created a website called iPoorly which shows information about acute illnesses for kids aged 0 to 5 in a simple, easy to understand and interactive way.

Our project client is the Acutely Sick Kid Safety Netting for Families (ASK SNIFF) research team. ASK SNIFF is a collaboration between parents & carers, higher education institutions, NHS Trusts and the charity or third sector in the UK. Our client point of contact is Associate Professor Sarah Neill, a nurse academic and researcher with over 20 years experience in higher education from the University of Northampton. The team has a wealth of parental, academic and clinical expertise relating to assessment and management of the acutely sick child. They have underwent numerous key stages to study and develop the final written content. The stages in which the team progressed through is visualised in the diagram below.

iPoorly is under the ASK SNIFF stage. The information for iPoorly will stem from the findings and researches of the previous stages.

Project Goals

The ASK SNIFF team has developed the written content for iPoorly. The content is based on national clinical guidelines and has been reviewed by parents and health professionals. The problem that the team faces now is the need to develop a prototype. The purpose of the web-app prototype is to provide parents of young children with video supported information resources to help families understand signs and symptoms of acute illness so that they can spot when their child is sick. These resources are also intended to support communication between families and health professionals.

Some of the main features of iPoorly include:

  • Information about acute symptoms in the form of text, videos and images to help parents and carers spot those symptoms in their child
  • Provide different information based on different age groups
  • Allow administrators to add new content or update existing content
  • Parents and carers can create an account for iPoorly to store profile details about their children
  • Users with an account can also create and keep a diary for each of their child

Requirements Gathering

On the 12th of October 2017, we met our client in person for the first time in UCL. We introduced ourselves to the client and got a brief specification of the project. We proceeded to discuss the project requirements in detail, by asking questions from a list that we had prepared prior to the meeting. We cleared our doubts and clarified the little details that we spotted in the project brief given. We managed to note down some key requirements of our project. Some of them are as follows:- Relevant sections as indicated in the content provided should be hyperlinked. Besides that, video links should be embedded as indicated in the content provided. Parents can set up a profile for each individual child or use the web app without doing so. Furthermore, background usage should be captured to enable the ASK SNIFF team to evaluate usage by geographical area, the number of times visited or downloaded, the parts of the website/app visited. Also, geolocation link to local services should be included. Finalised full list of requirements can be seen in the MoSCoW requirements section below.
