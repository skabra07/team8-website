Scenarios

Emily the Babysitter

Emily is a 20-year-old student who has a part time job as a babysitter. She is on her way to a new house to take care of Sara, a 2-year child. On her way to the house she logs on to the Ask Sniff website and checks all of the kids profile she is babysitting. She realizes that there is one child who she no longer takes care of and so removes that kid's profile from her account. When Emily reaches the house she meets Sara's parents. She logs in again to the Ask Sniff website and creates a profile for Sara. She enters Sara's name and date of birth. Now every time she is babysitting Sara she has Ask Sniff open with Sara's profile activated such that if Sara is not feeling well she can quickly get the relevant information to help understand what illness Sara might be facing and take the appropriate actions.

Mrs. Duffy the Single Mother

Mrs. Duffy returns home from work at 7 pm. As she enters the house she sees her daughter, Jane, who is 4 years old lying on the sofa. This was strange for Mrs. Duffy as Jane never usually slept at this time. As she got closer to Jane, she hears a very heavy and irregular breathing from Jane. This made Mrs. Duffy very worried but she has no clue as to what was happening to Jane. Due to the time she cannot call the nearby health care center for more clarification. Her only choice was to go to the emergency room at the nearest hospital, which is far and Jane would not be able to make the trip. She remembers her friend telling her about a website (Ask Sniff) which gives more information about illnesses. She quickly opens her laptop and goes to the website. She selects the age of her child (3-5) and sees the relevant information about a range of illnesses and their symptoms for kids aged between 3 and 5. She searches breathing and is presented with a page about symptoms to check for breathing problems as well as how to check them. There were also images/videos on what to look out for. The webpage also contained actions to take depending on which symptoms are visible. She takes the action provided by the website. This helped Jane to improve her breathing and her breathing became less loud and more regular.

Heuristic evaluation

We conducted heuristic evaluation for the design of our website as it was quick to carry out as well as easy to set up.We did consider using Cognitive Walkthrough as we could get a more detailed evaluation from the experts but we did not have any experts to act as the user and it was time consuming and expensive. The Heuristic evaluation is summarized below:

HeuristicProblemSolutionSeverity (0-4)
Recognition rather than recallWhen the user wanted to check the information on when to call 999, he was unable to find it on the webpage he was in and he did not want to leave the current page he was reading.Added a hyperlink on each page that opened a pop up modal to show the 999 information2
Aesthetic and minimalist designInformation in long flowing text that made it difficult to find certain informationDisplayed information in bullet points and key words were in bold2
Recognition rather than recallWhenever the user was trying to search for something they did not know what illnesses they could search forThe search bar displays illness depending on what the user is typing3
Error PreventionWhen a user typed in for an illness we did not have information on, the results page showed a blank screen and so the user did not know what was happeningAn error message is shown indicating that there is no information on the illness the user was searching for4
